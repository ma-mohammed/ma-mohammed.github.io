2025

  1. TRA
    A novel origin-destination-transfer model using mobile ticketing activations with seasonal passenger typology and spatial error correction
    M. Abdalazeem, T. Oke, and J. Oke
    Transportation Research Part A: Policy and Practice, 2025
    Under review
    Abs

    This paper presents an advanced ODX model integrating trip chaining, passenger typology, and spatial error correction to infer complete trip patterns, including transfers, in a regional transit system. The model leverages iterative proportional fitting (IPF) and machine learning techniques to scale partial AFC-based OD estimates to the full transit network population.

  2. DST
    A spatial typology analysis of crash characteristics across 2,480 census tracts in two New England states
    M. Abdalazeem and J. Oke
    Data Science for Transportation, 2025
    For resubmission
    Abs

    This study applies dimensionality reduction techniques, including UMAP, to extract latent spatial structures from crash data and uses Gaussian Mixture Modeling (GMM) to classify census tracts into distinct crash typologies based on historical patterns.

2024

  1. DST
    Enhanced seasonal typology-informed transit trip chaining via mobile boarding and survey data
    M. Abdalazeem and J. Oke
    Data Science for Transportation, 2024
    Abs DOI Bib HTML

    This study develops a trip chaining model incorporating passenger typology and seasonal variations to infer full passenger journeys using boarding-only AFC data. The model integrates a gradient boosting machine (GBM) for spatial error correction, reducing mean absolute error (MAE) by 70% and symmetric mean absolute percentage error (SMAPE) by 85%.

2023

  1. DST
    Extracting spatiotemporal bus passenger trip typologies from noisy mobile ticketing boarding data
    M. Abdalazeem and J. Oke
    Data Science for Transportation, 2023
    Abs DOI Bib HTML

    This paper develops a novel clustering framework using hierarchical clustering and dynamic time warping (DTW) to identify distinct spatiotemporal passenger typologies in the Pioneer Valley Transit Authority (PVTA) bus system. The method analyzes activation time series data from mobile ticketing transactions to infer behavioral patterns of different passenger groups.

2022

  1. IJTST
    Origin-destination inference in public transportation systems: A comprehensive review
    M. Abdalazeem and J. Oke
    International Journal of Transportation Science and Technology, 2022
    Abs DOI Bib HTML

    This paper presents a comprehensive review of state-of-the-art methods for origin-destination estimation in transit networks, identifying key advancements, data sources, and modeling techniques. The review synthesizes research trends in OD modeling, emphasizing the transition from survey-based methods to big data-driven techniques utilizing automated fare collection, automatic vehicle location, and mobile ticketing data.

2020

  1. MATEC
    Khartoum sky trains network: A study on implementing caterpillar trains concept in Khartoum
    M. A. Abdalazeem, O. Mohammed, M. Ibrahim, and 1 more author
    MATEC Web of Conferences, 2020
    Best Project Award Abs DOI Bib HTML

    Best Project Award from the Civil Engineering Department for innovative contributions to urban mobility

    This study develops a feasibility study for implementing a caterpillar-style elevated light rail system to alleviate traffic congestion in developing cities, using Khartoum, Sudan, as a case study. The research includes route optimization analysis using GIS and evaluation of financial and environmental impacts.
